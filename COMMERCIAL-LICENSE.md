## When you need a commercial license

The AGPL is generous, but it is **strong copyleft**. If you distribute CogMesh, or
offer it (or a derivative) to users **over a network** — including SaaS and internal
web services — the AGPL requires you to release the **complete corresponding source
code** of your application under the AGPL, to those users.

For many companies that is not acceptable. If any of these apply to you, you need a
commercial license:

- You want to build CogMesh into a **proprietary or closed-source** product.
- You provide a **hosted/SaaS** service using CogMesh and cannot open-source it.
- Your organization's policy **prohibits AGPL** code (common at large companies).
- You need to combine CogMesh with code you cannot release under the AGPL.
- You want **warranty, indemnification, or support** terms the AGPL does not provide.

A commercial license removes the AGPL copyleft obligations for your use, under terms
agreed in a separate signed agreement.
